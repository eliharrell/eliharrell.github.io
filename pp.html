Puppets & Props

a hand ~ 3D project

I was tapped by Horse Musicals, a troupe founded by Jake Schroeder and Harper Learmouth, to create puppets and props for original shows "Serve" (2024) and "Indigestion" (2025).

"SERVE"

3 foam puppets with fabric skin and faces, with fake fur tousled into hair.

In "Serve," the deceased husbands of the country club's tennis ladies are portrayed by shimmering metallic ghosts, with articulated heads and bodies made only of flowing flannel. 		Ghastly.
In the final act, the ghost husbands visit protagonist Sharon and reveal the truths of their terrible deaths at the hands of their tennis wives.
"INDIGESTION"

8 props: burger, airpod, chewed gum, key, crumb, knife, picket signs, small intestine, and Smithsonian Institute flag.

Props at XL scale because "Indigestion" takes place within the digestive tract.

From Horse Musicals: April the Appendix wants to make a difference, but there’s just one problem—she’s vestigial. INDIGESTION is a one-act about body parts with big personalities, navigating their roles in a body that’s far from perfect. Can April find her purpose, or will she just cause more trouble?

Indigestible: gum, a key, and a swallowed Airpod.

Ready for your appendectomy?

After bursting at record-breaking size, April floats happily ever after in a formaldehyde jar in the Smithsonian's medical collection.
Burger
24" x 24" x 18"

Two buns with soft exterior and rigid toasted interior, squeaky vinyl American cheese, brown bouclé patty. All parts are toroidal, connected with a "toothpick" axle for easy disassembly. 		Holding the axle.

Digestive system disassembles the burger.

Airpod
21" x 14" x 12" (approx. 17:1 scale)

Wire armature with acrylic-painted papier-mache skin.

No earwax used, although only Shen Yun's Vision Times was incorporated into the papier-mache. It is free. 		Balloon adds curvature between armature segments.
The armature was constructed without measurements, just close observation of the careful geometry of an Airpod. Rigid coathanger wire created cleaner ellipses, which form the main cross-sections of the pod, while softer aluminum was used to sculpt the complex linkages between them.
